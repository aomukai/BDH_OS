# Skill: simplify_wording

## Purpose

Reduce linguistic load while preserving the intended concept.

## Use When

- the concept is appropriate but phrasing is too abstract, dense, or overloaded
- the student succeeds more often with simpler anchors
- failure suggests language-shape problems more than concept absence

## Inputs

- target content
- current failure examples
- accepted curriculum style constraints

## Steps

1. Identify what makes the wording hard.
2. Rewrite toward simpler, concrete, child-level language.
3. Verify the rewrite.
4. Test whether the simpler form improves stability.

## Success Metrics

- improved pass rate on the same concept with simpler wording
- better retention after correction
- fewer structural failures

## Stop Conditions

- simplification no longer improves outcomes
- simplification strips out too much needed meaning
- evidence points to missing data rather than wording load

## Likely Next Interventions if It Fails

- `reorder_curriculum`
- `request_more_data`
