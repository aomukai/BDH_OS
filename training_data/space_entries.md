# Space Entries

[user]what does "on" mean?
[assistant]On describes a position where an object is supported by and touching the top surface of something else. A corgi sitting on a box is held up by the box’s hard surface. When something is on a surface, gravity pulls it down against that surface. Being on something is the opposite of being under it.

[user]what does "in" mean?
[assistant]In describes a position where an object is surrounded by something else or is inside a container. When a corgi is in a box, the walls of the box are all around it. Being in something means you are contained within its boundaries. You can be in a room, in a car, or in a pool of water. In is the opposite of out.

[user]what does "under" mean?
[assistant]Under describes a position where one object is directly below another. If a corgi is under a box, the box is above the corgi, acting like a roof or cover. Things that are under others are often hidden from sight or protected from the rain. Under is the opposite of on or over.

[user]what does "over" mean?
[assistant]Over describes a position where an object is at a higher level than something else, often moving across it. A corgi can jump over a box without touching it. Over implies there is a gap or space between the two objects. Over is the opposite of under.

[user]what does "behind" mean?
[assistant]Behind describes a position at the back of an object or person. If a corgi is behind a box, the box is between you and the dog, hiding it from your view. We use the word behind to describe things that are following others or are at the rear. Behind is the opposite of in front of.

[user]what does "in front of" mean?
[assistant]In front of describes a position at the forward part of an object or person. When a corgi is in front of a box, it is the first thing you see. This position is often used for things that are leading the way or facing toward you. In front of is the opposite of behind.

[user]what does "beside" mean?
[assistant]Beside describes a position at the side of an object or person. If a corgi is beside a box, it is standing next to one of the box’s walls. Being beside something means you are very close to it but not on top of it or inside it. Beside is another way of saying "next to."

[user]what does "between" mean?
[assistant]Between describes a position in the space that separates two objects or people. If a corgi is between two boxes, there is a box on its left and a box on its right. It is a middle position that connects two different things. Between helps us describe things that are caught in the middle.

[user]what does "near" mean?
[assistant]Near describes a position where one object is a short distance away from another. If a corgi is near a box, it is not touching it, but it is close enough to reach it quickly. Near is used to describe things that are in the same area but remain separate. Near is the opposite of far.

[user]what does "far" mean?
[assistant]Far describes a position where there is a great deal of distance between two objects. If a corgi is far from a box, it would have to walk or run for a long time to reach it. Far things look smaller to the eye because of the distance. Far is the opposite of near.

[user]what does "above" mean?
[assistant]Above describes a position where an object is at a higher level than another, but not necessarily touching it. A bird can fly above a box, or a lamp can hang above a table. Unlike "on," being above implies there is air or space between the two things. Above is the opposite of below.

[user]what does "below" mean?
[assistant]Below describes a position that is lower than something else. If a corgi is on the ground floor, it is below the roof of the house. Below is used to describe things that are further down toward the earth than the object we are looking at. Below is the opposite of above.

[user]what does "opposite" mean?
[assistant]Opposite describes a position where two things are facing each other with a space in between. If one corgi stands on one side of a box and another stands on the other side facing it, they are opposite each other. It is like looking into a mirror. We use opposite to describe things across a street or a table.

[user]what does "against" mean?
[assistant]Against describes a position where an object is touching and pressing into another for support. When a corgi leans against a box, it is using the box to keep itself from falling over. Being against something requires physical contact and force. You might lean a ladder against a wall or push a chair against a desk.
