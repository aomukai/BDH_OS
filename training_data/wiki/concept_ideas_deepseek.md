# deepseek:

Here is the category list for your elementary-school language model, organized by what a child actually talks about in daily life, play, school, and family settings.

---

CATEGORY: Daily routines & time blocks  
EXAMPLES: wake up, get dressed, eat breakfast, go to school, brush teeth, bedtime  
DEPENDS ON: morning/evening/night, bed, tooth, hand, face, shirt/pants/shoe, eat, water, milk  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Children structure their day around predictable routines, so these anchor time, actions, and objects together.

---

CATEGORY: Family roles & kinship  
EXAMPLES: mom, dad, sibling, grandparent, aunt, uncle, cousin  
DEPENDS ON: home, love (feeling, implied)  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Family is a child’s primary social world; these terms appear constantly in conversation and stories.

---

CATEGORY: Friends & social play  
EXAMPLES: friend, share, take turns, play together, invite, argue, make up  
DEPENDS ON: happy/sad, ball, doll, block, rule, problem/solution  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Peer interaction is central to elementary school; requires cooperation, conflict resolution, and turn-taking.

---

CATEGORY: School activities & places  
EXAMPLES: classroom, teacher, desk, read, write, draw, recess, lunchbox, homework  
DEPENDS ON: book, paper, crayon, table, chair, eat, play  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: School occupies most of a child’s waking hours; needs vocabulary for learning spaces and tasks.

---

CATEGORY: Chores & responsibilities  
EXAMPLES: clean up, put away, set the table, sweep, water plants, feed pet  
DEPENDS ON: broom, bucket, cup/plate/bowl, spoon, dog/cat/fish, water, plant (from natural world, implied)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Children are expected to help at home; teaches cause/effect (clean vs dirty) and part/whole of household tasks.

---

CATEGORY: Emotions & body feelings  
EXAMPLES: scared, excited, worried, frustrated, proud, lonely  
DEPENDS ON: happy/sad, tired, hungry/thirsty, belly, head, face, friend, home  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Basic emotional literacy is required for self-regulation and describing internal states beyond tired/hungry.

---

CATEGORY: Health & minor injuries  
EXAMPLES: cut, bruise, scrape, fever, cough, sneeze, bandage, rest  
DEPENDS ON: skin, bone, hand/foot, blood (implied), pain (implied), sleep (from daily routines)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Children get hurt and sick often; need to report symptoms and understand basic first aid.

---

CATEGORY: Professions (community helpers)  
EXAMPLES: doctor, nurse, police officer, firefighter, mail carrier, bus driver, librarian  
DEPENDS ON: car, bus, book, home, school, road, fire, sick/injured (implied)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: These adults appear regularly in a child’s environment; also supports “when I grow up” conversations.

---

CATEGORY: Holidays & celebrations  
EXAMPLES: birthday, gift, party, cake, holiday, celebration, tradition  
DEPENDS ON: candle, song (implied), friend, family, happy, eat, candle (from objects)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Holidays are emotionally salient and structure the year; introduces cultural and family-specific knowledge.

---

CATEGORY: Weather & seasonal changes (processes)  
EXAMPLES: forecast, temperature, melt, freeze, dry, wet, cloudy, clear sky  
DEPENDS ON: sun, rain, snow, wind, ice, frost, hot/cold (implied from feeling, but needs expansion)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Children plan clothing and activities around weather; teaches change over time (ice melts) and cause/effect (sun dries puddles).

---

CATEGORY: Simple measurement & comparison  
EXAMPLES: bigger than, smaller than, longer, shorter, heavier, lighter, faster, slower, hotter, colder  
DEPENDS ON: size (already in abstract), number (0-10), same/different, time (faster/slower)  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Used constantly in play (“my tower is taller”), sharing (“I have more”), and science.

---

CATEGORY: Position & direction (extended)  
EXAMPLES: left, right, up, down, forward, backward, through, around, across, next to  
DEPENDS ON: on/in/under/over/near/far/between (already present)  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Needed for giving and following directions, playground games, and describing layouts.

---

CATEGORY: Money & trade  
EXAMPLES: buy, sell, cost, penny, nickel, dime, quarter, dollar, pay, save, earn  
DEPENDS ON: number (0-10), addition/subtraction, want (implied), need (implied)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Children encounter money at stores, allowance, and lemonade stands; teaches exchange and value.

---

CATEGORY: Cooking & food preparation  
EXAMPLES: mix, stir, pour, measure, peel, cut, bake, boil, fry, taste, recipe  
DEPENDS ON: spoon, bowl, pot, cup, water, milk, egg, flour (from bag of flour), sugar (implied), heat (implied)  
SEQUENCE: late  
REASON: Cooking ties together measurement, sequence (first/then/last), change (liquid to solid), and safety.

---

CATEGORY: Safety & rules  
EXAMPLES: danger, careful, hot, sharp, poison, stranger, emergency, call for help, hold hands, look both ways  
DEPENDS ON: road, car, fire, knife (implied from cut), home, parent, doctor  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Critical for real-world behavior; requires understanding possible/impossible and necessary from abstract logic.

---

CATEGORY: Growing & life cycles (animals/plants)  
EXAMPLES: seed, sprout, grow, bloom, die, hatch, larva, pupa, adult, baby animal  
DEPENDS ON: tree, flower (implied from garden), butterfly, frog (tadpole already present), bird (egg already present)  
SEQUENCE: late  
REASON: Elementary science curriculum core; teaches change, sequence, and cause/effect in biology.

---

CATEGORY: Games & sports rules  
EXAMPLES: tag, hide and seek, jump rope, catch, kick, throw, score, team, win, lose, fair, cheat  
DEPENDS ON: ball, run (implied from movement), friend, rule, happy/sad, number  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Playground and PE vocabulary is essential for inclusion and understanding fairness.

---

CATEGORY: Art & creative expression  
EXAMPLES: draw, color, paint, glue, cut, fold, clay, craft, decorate, erase  
DEPENDS ON: paper, crayon, scissors (implied), stick, block, color, shape  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Daily classroom activity; requires fine motor action verbs and result descriptions.

---

CATEGORY: Bathroom & hygiene  
EXAMPLES: toilet, sink, soap, towel, wash, flush, brush (teeth), comb (hair), tissue, blow nose  
DEPENDS ON: hand, face, tooth, hair, water, soap (implied)  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Personal care routines are private but necessary for communication with adults and peers.

---

CATEGORY: Sleep & dreams  
EXAMPLES: sleepy, yawn, blanket, pillow, dream, nightmare, quiet, dark, light (as in lamp on/off)  
DEPENDS ON: bed, night, tired, light, moon, star (for night associations)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Sleep issues (bad dreams, bedtime resistance) are common conversation topics for young children.

---

CATEGORY: Wants, needs & preferences  
EXAMPLES: want, need, like, don’t like, choose, favorite, rather, prefer  
DEPENDS ON: happy/sad, same/different, fact/opinion (from abstract)  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Expressing preferences is foundational for identity and negotiation (“I want the red cup”).

---

CATEGORY: Permission & requests  
EXAMPLES: can I, may I, please, thank you, sorry, excuse me, allow, forbid, ask, tell  
DEPENDS ON: rule, necessary/polite (implied from social norm)  
SEQUENCE: early  
REASON: Politeness and requesting are daily language functions; links to social consequences.

---

CATEGORY: Stories & narratives (sequence words)  
EXAMPLES: once upon a time, first, next, then, finally, later, before, after, suddenly, at the end  
DEPENDS ON: begin/middle/end, cause/effect, time (past/present/future)  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Children retell events and make up stories; these discourse markers organize thought.

---

CATEGORY: Learning & memory  
EXAMPLES: learn, remember, forget, practice, try again, make a mistake, figure out, know, guess  
DEPENDS ON: problem/solution, goal, change, possible/impossible  
SEQUENCE: late  
REASON: Metacognition emerges in elementary years; needed for school success (“I forgot my homework”).

---

CATEGORY: Machines & simple mechanisms  
EXAMPLES: pull, push, turn, spin, roll, slide, lift, drop, catch, bounce  
DEPENDS ON: wheel, lever, rope, hook, screw, hammer, block, ball  
SEQUENCE: late  
REASON: Physics for children (playground swings, doors, bikes); teaches force and motion.

---

CATEGORY: Digital & media objects  
EXAMPLES: phone, tablet, computer, TV, remote, game, video, song, button (touch), screen, battery  
DEPENDS ON: button, light, sound (implied), picture (implied from book/crayon)  
SEQUENCE: late  
REASON: Modern children interact with screens daily; missing from original object list.

---

CATEGORY: Time cycles (days of week, months, seasons)  
EXAMPLES: Monday–Sunday, January–December, spring/summer/fall/winter, yesterday/today/tomorrow, weekend, weekday  
DEPENDS ON: day/week/month/year/season, morning/afternoon/evening/night  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Required for scheduling (“We have gym on Tuesday”) and understanding calendar.

---

CATEGORY: Physical states & transformations  
EXAMPLES: melt, freeze, boil, evaporate, dissolve, break, fix, build, bend, stretch, tear, repair  
DEPENDS ON: ice, water, wood, stone, fire, heat (implied), cold (implied)  
SEQUENCE: late  
REASON: Explains everyday changes (butter melting, paper tearing, snowman melting); foundational for science.

---

CATEGORY: Communication acts  
EXAMPLES: say, tell, ask, answer, whisper, shout, explain, promise, lie, truth, joke  
DEPENDS ON: truth, fact/opinion, friend, parent, teacher  
SEQUENCE: middle  
REASON: Metalanguage for talking about language itself; crucial for social negotiation and literacy.

---

That covers the main gaps. The sequence moves from **early** (daily survival and play) to **middle** (school, friends, feelings, rules) to **late** (processes, cycles, transformations, and metacognition).
