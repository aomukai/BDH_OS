# Grok:
  
  CATEGORY: Family Relationships
EXAMPLES: mother, father, brother, sister, grandmother, grandfather
DEPENDS ON: home, family not present but body (face hair), time (past present)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Family members form the foundation of every child's daily conversations about who lives together, helps with tasks, and shares feelings.

CATEGORY: Friendships and Peer Interactions
EXAMPLES: friend, best friend, classmate, playdate, sharing, taking turns
DEPENDS ON: abstract (same different, rule, feeling happy sad), body (hand for holding hands)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Children constantly talk about who they play with, who is nice or mean, and how to get along with others at school or park.

CATEGORY: Daily Routines
EXAMPLES: wake up, get dressed, brush teeth, eat breakfast, go to bed, pack backpack
DEPENDS ON: time (morning afternoon night day), clothing, body (tooth hand), food (milk bread)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Kids describe what happens every morning and evening, creating predictable stories about their own lives.

CATEGORY: School Life
EXAMPLES: teacher, student, classroom, recess, homework, school bus
DEPENDS ON: book, desk, crayon, paper, bus, time (day week)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: School is the main place children spend their days, so they need words to talk about learning, teachers, and friends there.

CATEGORY: Professions and Community Roles
EXAMPLES: doctor, firefighter, police officer, mail carrier, baker, farmer
DEPENDS ON: farm, tools (hammer shovel), vehicles (truck car), places (road farm)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Children ask about what grown-ups do all day and see these helpers in their neighborhood every week.

CATEGORY: Health and Body Systems
EXAMPLES: heart beats, lungs breathe, stomach digests, blood, muscles, skeleton
DEPENDS ON: body (belly hand foot bone head), food (milk bread)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Explains how the body works inside as a connected system when kids feel tired, strong, or wonder why they breathe.

CATEGORY: Illness and Medical Care
EXAMPLES: sick, fever, cough, medicine, bandage, hospital visit
DEPENDS ON: health and body systems, doctor (from professions), food (soup water)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Kids frequently tell parents or teachers when they feel unwell or describe trips to the doctor.

CATEGORY: Expanded Emotions
EXAMPLES: angry, scared, excited, proud, lonely, disappointed
DEPENDS ON: feeling (happy sad tired hungry thirsty), face (eye mouth), abstract (cause effect)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Allows children to name stronger or mixed feelings that come up during play, school, or family moments.

CATEGORY: Physical Movement and Actions
EXAMPLES: run, jump, climb, kick, throw, dance, swim
DEPENDS ON: body (leg arm foot hand), vehicles (bike), places (field playground implied via meadow field)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Describes how children move their bodies during play and sports, a huge part of recess and after-school talk.

CATEGORY: Games and Play Activities
EXAMPLES: hide and seek, tag, board game, playground equipment, rules, winner
DEPENDS ON: ball, doll, sandbox, seesaw, abstract (rule order goal problem solution)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Playtime rules and games are what children negotiate and argue about most often with friends.

CATEGORY: Communication and Language
EXAMPLES: speak, listen, read aloud, write, ask question, tell story
DEPENDS ON: mouth, ear, book, paper, crayon, abstract (if then and or)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Children practice talking, asking questions, and sharing stories every single day at home and school.

CATEGORY: Measurement and Comparison
EXAMPLES: bigger smaller, heavier lighter, taller shorter, faster slower, half full
DEPENDS ON: number (0-10), size, shape, partitives (cup of bowl of), abstract (same different)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Kids compare toys, snacks, heights, and speeds constantly when playing or helping at home.

CATEGORY: Time and Schedules
EXAMPLES: clock, calendar, yesterday today tomorrow, minute, hour, bedtime routine
DEPENDS ON: time (past present future day week month season morning night)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Helps children understand when things happen, wait for events, and plan their day.

CATEGORY: Natural Cycles and Processes
EXAMPLES: day-night cycle, seasons changing, water cycle, seed grows into plant, caterpillar to butterfly
DEPENDS ON: sun moon star time (season day night), natural world (rain snow tree river), animals (tadpole butterfly)
SEQUENCE: late
REASON: Explains the repeating changes children notice in weather, plants, and animals throughout the year.

CATEGORY: Cooking and Meal Preparation
EXAMPLES: cook, bake, stir, pour, mix, recipe
DEPENDS ON: food and drink, objects (pot spoon bowl cup), fire, time (morning evening)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Children help in the kitchen or talk about favorite meals being made at home.

CATEGORY: Manners and Social Rules
EXAMPLES: please, thank you, excuse me, share, wait your turn, help others
DEPENDS ON: abstract (rule order goal problem solution, feeling), family relationships
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Parents and teachers remind children about polite words and fair behavior many times each day.

CATEGORY: Safety and Emergency Awareness
EXAMPLES: safe, dangerous, helmet, seatbelt, stop look listen, call for help
DEPENDS ON: vehicles (bike car), road, clothing (hat coat), abstract (rule possible impossible)
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Children learn and repeat safety rules for crossing streets, riding bikes, and staying safe.

CATEGORY: Holidays and Celebrations
EXAMPLES: birthday, party, gift, cake, candle, special day
DEPENDS ON: time (year season), food (cake cookie), family relationships, objects (candle implied via fire light)
SEQUENCE: middle
REASON: Holidays and birthdays are the most exciting events children look forward to and recount afterward.

CATEGORY: Growth and Life Stages
EXAMPLES: baby, toddler, child, grown-up, getting bigger, learning new skills
DEPENDS ON: time (past future), body, family relationships, natural cycles
SEQUENCE: late
REASON: Helps children understand their own growth and how family members change over time.

CATEGORY: Imagination and Creative Play
EXAMPLES: pretend, make-believe, story character, superhero, invent a game, draw a picture
DEPENDS ON: book, doll, crayon, paper, abstract (possible impossible change), emotions
SEQUENCE: early
REASON: Pretend play and storytelling fill children's free time and fuel their conversations about what "could happen."
