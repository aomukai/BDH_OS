# Directions and Navigation Entries

[user]what does "left" mean?
[assistant]Left names one side of the body or one side of a place. If you turn left, you go toward the left side. People use left to follow directions and find places. Left is the opposite of right.

[user]what does "right" mean?
[assistant]Right names one side of the body or one side of a place. If you turn right, you go toward the right side. People use right to follow directions and find places. Right is the opposite of left.

[user]what does "up" mean?
[assistant]Up means toward a higher place. A bird can fly up, and a child can walk up stairs. Up shows a direction that goes higher. Up is the opposite of down.

[user]what does "down" mean?
[assistant]Down means toward a lower place. Rain falls down, and a child can walk down stairs. Down shows a direction that goes lower. Down is the opposite of up.

[user]what does "forward" mean?
[assistant]Forward means toward the front. When you walk forward, you move ahead instead of back. People move forward when they follow a path or go where they are facing. Forward is the opposite of backward.

[user]what does "backward" mean?
[assistant]Backward means toward the back. When you step backward, you move away from what is in front of you. Backward movement goes the opposite way from forward movement. Backward is the opposite of forward.

[user]what does it mean to turn?
[assistant]To turn means to change direction. A person can turn left, turn right, or turn around. Turning helps people follow a road, path, or set of directions. To turn is not the same as to go straight.

[user]what does "go straight" mean?
[assistant]Go straight means keep moving ahead without turning. If a road keeps going forward, you can go straight on it. People say go straight when they give directions to a place. Go straight is not the same as to turn.

[user]what is a map?
[assistant]A map is a picture that shows where places are. A map can show roads, rivers, buildings, parks, or other places. People use maps to find places and choose where to go. A map is not the place itself.

[user]what is a route?
[assistant]A route is the way from one place to another. A bus has a route, and a person walking home can follow a route. A route may use roads, paths, or turns along the way. A route is not a map.

[user]what is an address?
[assistant]An address is the name of a place written in a way that helps people find it. A home, school, or store can have an address. People use an address for letters, visits, and directions. An address is not a route.
