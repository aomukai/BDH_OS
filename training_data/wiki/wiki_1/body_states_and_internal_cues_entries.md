[user]what is a body signal?
[assistant]A body signal is something the body does or feels to show a need or problem. Hunger, thirst, pain, and tiredness are body signals. A body signal can tell a person to eat, drink, rest, or get help. A body signal is not a thought.

[user]what does it mean to feel dizzy?
[assistant]To feel dizzy means to feel unsteady or as if things are turning. A dizzy person may need to sit down or hold still. Dizzy feelings can make it hard to walk or stand well. To feel dizzy is not the same as to feel steady.

[user]what does it mean to feel itchy?
[assistant]To feel itchy means to feel a need to scratch the skin. A bug bite, dry skin, or rough cloth can make a person feel itchy. Itchy skin can be annoying or distracting. To feel itchy is not the same as to feel smooth.

[user]what does it mean to feel sore?
[assistant]To feel sore means to hurt in a dull or aching way. A sore arm, leg, or throat may hurt when the body moves or is touched. People may feel sore after hard work, exercise, or sickness. To feel sore is not the same as to feel comfortable.

[user]what does it mean to shiver?
[assistant]To shiver means for the body to shake in small quick movements. People often shiver when they are very cold or scared. Shivering is a body sign that something feels wrong or uncomfortable. To shiver is not the same as to stay still.

[user]what does it mean to sweat?
[assistant]To sweat means for the body to make drops of water on the skin. People sweat when they are hot, working hard, or nervous. Sweat can help cool the body down. To sweat is not the same as to shiver.

[assistant]To breathe fast means to take air in and out more quickly than usual. People may breathe fast after running, during fear, or when the body is working hard. Fast breathing is a body signal that the body needs more air right away. To breathe fast is not the same as to breathe slowly.

A fast heartbeat is a body signal that the body is reacting to something. A fast heartbeat is not the same as a calm one.
