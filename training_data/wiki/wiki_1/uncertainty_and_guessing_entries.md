# Uncertainty and Guessing Entries

[user]what is uncertainty?
[Ninereeds]Uncertainty is the state of not knowing for sure. A person may feel uncertainty when the answer is not clear yet. Uncertainty can happen during questions, choices, or searches. People often use careful words during uncertainty. Uncertainty is not the same as certainty.

[user]what does maybe mean?
[Ninereeds]Maybe is a word people use when something could be true or could happen, but the speaker is not sure. A child may say maybe it is in the bag or maybe we will go later. Maybe shows possibility without certainty. The word keeps the answer open. Maybe is not the same as definitely.

[user]what does probably mean?
[Ninereeds]Probably is a word people use when something seems likely, but is still not fully sure. A child may say the book is probably in the classroom or we will probably go after lunch. Probably is stronger than maybe, but weaker than knowing. The word shows a careful guess. Probably is not the same as certainly.

[user]what does I guess mean?
[Ninereeds]I guess is something people say before giving an uncertain answer. A child may say I guess it is under the bed or I guess blue is fine. The phrase shows the speaker is answering without full certainty. I guess can sound thoughtful, unsure, or tentative. I guess is not the same as I know.

[user]what does not sure mean?
[Ninereeds]Not sure means the speaker does not know clearly yet. A child may say I am not sure where the toy is or I am not sure which one is right. The phrase shows honest uncertainty. Not sure can invite more looking, thinking, or asking. Not sure is not the same as certain.

[user]what does could be mean?
[Ninereeds]Could be means something is one possible answer or result. A child may say it could be in the car or this could be the right door. The phrase points to possibility, not proof. Could be leaves room for another answer. Could be is not the same as must be.

[user]what does might mean?
[Ninereeds]Might is a word people use when something is possible but not guaranteed. A child may say it might rain later or the pencil might be in the desk. Might often sounds cautious and open. The word helps people avoid sounding too sure. Might is not the same as will.

[user]what does I wonder mean?
[Ninereeds]I wonder is something people say when they are curious and do not know the answer yet. A child may say I wonder where the cat went or I wonder what will happen next. The phrase can show curiosity as well as uncertainty. I wonder often comes before a question or a guess. I wonder is not the same as I know.
