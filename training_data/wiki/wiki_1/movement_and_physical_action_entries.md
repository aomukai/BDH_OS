# Movement and Physical Action Entries

[user]what is movement?
[assistant]Movement means changing place or position. People, animals, water, and many other things can show movement. Movement can be fast or slow, big or small. Movement is not the same as staying still.

[user]what is exercise?
[assistant]Exercise is body movement done to make the body stronger or healthier. People exercise by walking, running, stretching, jumping, or playing active games. Exercise helps the body work well and stay active. Exercise is not the same as staying still.

[user]what is balance?
[assistant]Balance means keeping the body steady and not falling over. People use balance when they stand, walk, hop, or ride a bicycle. Good balance helps the body stay in control. Balance is not the same as falling.

[user]what does it mean to stretch?
[assistant]To stretch means to make part of the body longer or reach it out. People stretch arms, legs, backs, and fingers. Stretching can help the body get ready to move or feel less tight. To stretch is not the same as to bend.

[user]what does it mean to kick?
[assistant]To kick means to hit or move something with the foot or leg. People kick balls in games and sports. A kick can be soft or strong. To kick is not the same as to throw.

[user]what does it mean to bounce?
[assistant]To bounce means to hit a surface and come back up. A ball bounces on the ground when it is dropped or thrown. People can also bounce up and down with their bodies. To bounce is not the same as to roll.

[user]what does it mean to spin?
[assistant]To spin means to turn around and around. A top spins, and a person can spin in place. Spinning is a kind of movement that goes in a circle. To spin is not the same as to slide.

[user]what does it mean to dance?
[assistant]To dance means to move the body in a lively or patterned way. People dance to music, for fun, or as part of a group activity. Dancing may include steps, turns, jumps, or arm movements. To dance is not the same as to spin.
