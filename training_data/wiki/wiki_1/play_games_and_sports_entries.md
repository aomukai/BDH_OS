# Play, Games, and Sports Entries

[user]what is play?
[Ninereeds]Play is an activity people do for fun, movement, or imagination. Children play alone or with other people. Play can include games, sports, pretending, building, or drawing. Play helps children move, learn, and enjoy time together. Play is not the same as homework.

[user]what is a game?
[Ninereeds]A game is a kind of play with rules or a shared goal. A game may use cards, a ball, a board, or only people's bodies and voices. Many games have turns, teams, or ways to win. Games help people practice fairness, listening, and planning. A game is not the same as a lesson.

[user]what is a sport?
[Ninereeds]A sport is a game or activity that uses body movement and skill. Many sports use running, throwing, kicking, jumping, or teamwork. Sports may be played for fun, practice, or competition. A sport often has clear rules and a score. A sport is not the same as sitting still.

[user]what is a team?
[Ninereeds]A team is a group of people working or playing together toward one goal. A team may play a sport, solve a challenge, or take part in a game. Team members help each other and share roles. Teams often need listening and cooperation. A team is not the same as one person alone.

[user]what is a score?
[Ninereeds]A score is the number that shows how many points someone or a team has. Many games and sports use a score to show progress or the result. Players may watch the score during the game. A score helps people know who is ahead. A score is not the same as a rule.

[user]what does it mean to win?
[Ninereeds]To win means to finish with the best result in a game, race, or contest. A player or team may win by getting more points or by reaching the goal first. Winning can feel exciting, but it is only one part of play. People can still play fairly whether they win or lose. To win is the opposite of to lose.

[user]what does it mean to lose?
[Ninereeds]To lose means not to finish with the best result in a game, race, or contest. A player or team may lose when another player or team gets more points or reaches the goal first. Losing can feel disappointing, but people can still act kindly and keep trying. Losing is part of many games and sports. To lose is the opposite of to win.

[user]what does it mean to cheat?
[Ninereeds]To cheat means to break the rules in order to get an unfair result. A child may cheat by skipping a turn, moving a game piece too far, or lying about a score. Cheating can spoil trust and fun. Fair play matters more than winning by cheating. To cheat is not the same as to play fair.

[user]what is tag?
[Ninereeds]Tag is a running game where one player tries to touch another player. The player who gets touched may become the next one to chase. Tag uses movement, speed, and simple rules. Children often play tag outside during recess or free time. Tag is not the same as hide and seek.

[user]what is hide and seek?
[Ninereeds]Hide and seek is a game where one player looks for other players who are hiding. One person counts while the others hide first. The game uses waiting, looking, and finding. Hide and seek can be played inside some places or outside in safe places. Hide and seek is not the same as tag.
