# Sensory Experiences Entries

[user]what is a sound?
[assistant]A sound is something heard with the ears. Voices, music, wind, and animal calls are all sounds. Some sounds are loud and some are quiet. A sound is not a color.

[user]what is loud?
[assistant]Loud means a sound is strong and easy to hear. Thunder, shouting, and a drum can be loud. Loud sounds can surprise people or hurt the ears if they are too strong. Loud is the opposite of quiet.

[user]what is quiet?
[assistant]Quiet means there is very little sound. A quiet room, a quiet voice, or a quiet night is not loud. Quiet can help people rest, listen, or think. Quiet is the opposite of loud.

[user]what is noisy?
[assistant]Noisy means full of sound. A busy road, a loud classroom, or a barking yard can be noisy. Noisy places can make it hard to hear one thing clearly. Noisy is not the same as quiet.

[user]what is silent?
[assistant]Silent means without sound. A silent room has no voices, music, or other noise. Silent is stronger than quiet. Silent is the opposite of noisy.

[user]what is bright?
[assistant]Bright means giving a lot of light or being full of light. The sun, a lamp, or a shiny fire can be bright. Bright light can make things easy to see. Bright is the opposite of dim.

[user]what is dim?
[assistant]Dim means not very bright. A dim room has only a little light. Dim light can make it harder to see clearly. Dim is the opposite of bright.

[user]what is sticky?
[assistant]Sticky means something clings when you touch it. Honey, syrup, or wet glue can feel sticky. Sticky things may stay on fingers or other surfaces. Sticky is not the same as dry.

[user]what is sweet?
[assistant]Sweet is a taste like honey, sugar, or many ripe fruits. Candy, cake, and some drinks can taste sweet. Sweet is one kind of taste people notice with the mouth and tongue. Sweet is not sour.

[user]what is sour?
[assistant]Sour is a sharp taste like lemon or some green fruit. Sour things can make a person's mouth tighten. Sour is different from sweet. Sour is not sweet.

[user]what is a bang?
[assistant]A bang is a sudden loud sound. A door slamming, thunder, or something falling hard can make a bang. A bang happens quickly and strongly. A bang is not a melody.

[user]what is a squeak?
[assistant]A squeak is a small high sound. A mouse, a toy, or a door can make a squeak. Squeaks are usually short and light. A squeak is not a roar.

[user]what is a roar?
[assistant]A roar is a strong deep loud sound. A lion, a large engine, or crashing water can make a roar. A roar is bigger and heavier than a squeak. A roar is not a squeak.

[user]what is a chirp?
[assistant]A chirp is a small quick sound often made by birds or insects. Many little birds chirp in the morning. Chirps are light and short. A chirp is not a roar.

[user]what is a melody?
[assistant]A melody is a line of musical sounds that go together. A song often has a melody people can remember and sing. A melody is part of music, not just one sound alone. A melody is not noise.
