# Imagination and Pretend Play Entries

[user]what is imagination?
[assistant]Imagination is when a person makes pictures, ideas, or stories in the mind. A child may imagine a dragon, a castle, or a trip to the moon. Imagination helps people think of new games and new stories. Imagination is not the same as a fact.

[user]what does pretend mean?
[assistant]Pretend means to act as if something is true for play. A child may pretend to be a doctor, a shopkeeper, or a dragon. Pretend is part of play and story making. Pretend is not the same as real.

[user]what is pretend play?
[assistant]Pretend play is play where children act out ideas, roles, or stories. Children may pretend to cook, teach, drive, or care for a baby doll. Pretend play helps children use words, actions, and ideas together. Pretend play is not the same as homework.

[user]what is make-believe?
[assistant]Make-believe is a kind of pretend play with imagined people, places, or events. A child may play in a make-believe castle or a make-believe store. Make-believe lets children create a world that is not really there. Make-believe is not the same as real.

[user]what is dress up?
[assistant]Dress up is putting on clothes or costumes to pretend to be someone or something else. A child may wear a cape to pretend to be a hero. Dress up helps children show a role with clothing. Dress up is not the same as homework.

[user]what is role play?
[assistant]Role play is pretend play where each person acts as a certain role. One child may be a doctor while another child is a patient. Role play helps children take turns and act out a scene together. Role play is not the same as homework.

[user]what is an imaginary friend?
[assistant]An imaginary friend is a pretend friend made in a child's mind. A child may talk to the imaginary friend or include the friend in play. An imaginary friend can be part of imagination and pretend play. An imaginary friend is not real.

[user]what is symbolic play?
[assistant]Symbolic play is pretend play where one thing stands for another thing. A block may stand for a phone, or a stick may stand for a spoon. Symbolic play helps children use objects in flexible ways. Symbolic play is not the same as using an object only in the usual way.

[user]what is a game of pretend?
[assistant]A game of pretend is a play activity built around imagination and make-believe. Children may play house, school, store, or space trip in a game of pretend. A game of pretend often has roles, actions, and a simple story. A game of pretend is not the same as homework.
