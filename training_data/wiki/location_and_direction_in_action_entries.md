# Location and Direction in Action Entries

[user]what does come here mean?
[assistant]Come here means move to the place where the speaker is. A parent or teacher may say come here to call a child closer. The phrase gives a direction linked to the speaker's place. Come here often asks for movement right now. Come here is not the same as go there.

[user]what does go there mean?
[assistant]Go there means move to another place away from the speaker. A child may hear go there when being told where to stand or sit. The phrase points toward a different location. Go there is a short direction for movement. Go there is not the same as come here.

[user]what does bring it to me mean?
[assistant]Bring it to me means pick something up and carry it to the speaker. A teacher may say bring it to me about a paper or a book. The phrase joins movement with an object and a person. It tells both what to move and where to bring it. Bring it to me is not the same as take it away.

[user]what does put it on the table mean?
[assistant]Put it on the table means place something so it rests on top of the table. A child may hear this during cleanup, art, or class work. The phrase gives both an action and a final place. It helps objects go where they belong. Put it on the table is not the same as put it under the table.

[user]what does take it outside mean?
[assistant]Take it outside means carry something from inside to an outdoor place. A person may say this about trash, shoes, or a toy for outdoor play. The phrase links an object with a direction of movement. It changes the place where the object is. Take it outside is not the same as bring it inside.

[user]what does move it over mean?
[assistant]Move it over means shift something a short distance to one side. A parent or teacher may say this to make more space on a table or bench. The phrase gives a small direction, not a whole trip to another room. Moving it over changes where the thing sits. Move it over is not the same as leave it there.

[user]what does set it down mean?
[assistant]Set it down means place something onto a surface in a careful way. A child may set a bag down, set a book down, or set a cup down. The phrase often means do not keep holding it. Setting something down helps free the hands or place the object safely. Set it down is not the same as pick it up.

[user]what does point to it mean?
[assistant]Point to it means use a finger or hand to show the thing someone should notice. A teacher may say point to it during a lesson or a game. The phrase helps direct another person's eyes. Pointing shows a place or object without moving it. Point to it is not the same as pick it up.
