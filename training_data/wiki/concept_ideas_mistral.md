# Mistral:

Here’s a comprehensive list of wiki categories designed to bridge the gaps in your model’s knowledge, focusing on what children aged 6–10 actually talk about and need to understand for daily conversation. The categories are organized to build on existing knowledge and introduce relational, abstract, and social concepts progressively.

---

**CATEGORY:** Daily Routines and Activities
**EXAMPLES:** waking up, brushing teeth, getting dressed, eating meals, going to school, bedtime
**DEPENDS ON:** time (morning/afternoon/evening/night), body (hand, foot, mouth, tooth), clothing (shirt, pants, shoe, sock), food and drink (apple, milk, bread)
**SEQUENCE:** early
**REASON:** Children frequently discuss their daily activities, and this category anchors conversations about personal experiences and schedules.

---

**CATEGORY:** Family and Social Relationships
**EXAMPLES:** mother, father, sibling, friend, teacher, neighbor
**DEPENDS ON:** body (face, hand, eye), places (home, school), abstract (feeling, logic)
**SEQUENCE:** early
**REASON:** Social relationships are central to a child’s world and provide context for emotions, rules, and communication.

---

**CATEGORY:** School Life and Learning

**DEPENDS ON:** objects and tools (book, paper, crayon, desk), abstract (number, counting, shape, color)
**SEQUENCE:** early
**REASON:** School is a major part of a child’s life, and this category supports discussions about learning, rules, and social interactions.

**CATEGORY:** Communication and Language
