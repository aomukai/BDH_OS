# Mathematical Concepts Entries

[user]what is a number?
[assistant]A number is a symbol used to count, measure, or label things. Numbers help us say how many of something there are. We use numbers every day to tell time, count objects, and measure distances. Numbers can be small, like one or two, or very large. A number is not a letter.

[user]what is zero?
[assistant]Zero is a number that means there is nothing to count. It is written as the symbol 0. Zero comes before one when you count. If you eat all the apples in a bowl, the bowl has zero apples left. Zero is not the same as one.

[user]what is counting?
[assistant]Counting is the act of saying numbers in order to find out how many things there are. We count by saying one, two, three, and so on. Counting helps us know if we have enough of something. You can count objects like apples, chairs, or people. Counting is not guessing.

[user]what is addition?
[assistant]Addition is the process of combining two or more numbers to get a larger number. When you add two and three, you get five. The result of addition is called the sum. Addition uses the plus sign. Addition is not subtraction.

[user]what is subtraction?
[assistant]Subtraction is the process of taking one number away from another. When you subtract two from five, you get three. The result of subtraction is called the difference. Subtraction uses the minus sign. Subtraction is not addition.

[user]what is equal?
[assistant]Equal means the same in amount, size, or value. When two groups have the same number of things, they are equal. The equal sign looks like two short horizontal lines placed one above the other. Four plus one is equal to five. Equal is not more than or less than.

[user]what is more than?
[assistant]More than means a greater number or amount of something. If there are five birds and three cats, there are more birds than cats. We use the words more than to compare two groups. Having more means having a greater amount. More than is not less than.

[user]what is less than?
[assistant]Less than means a smaller number or amount of something. If there are two dogs and six fish, there are fewer dogs than fish. We say the number of dogs is less than the number of fish. Less than is the opposite of more than. Less than is not equal.

[user]what is a half?
[assistant]A half is one of two equal parts of something. If you cut an apple into two equal pieces, each piece is a half. Two halves make a whole. Half of four is two. A half is not a whole.

[user]what is a shape?
[assistant]A shape is the form or outline of an object. Every object has a shape, from round to square to pointed. Shapes can be drawn on paper or found in the world around us. The shape of something helps us recognize and describe it. A shape is not a color.

[user]what is a circle?
[assistant]A circle is a round, flat shape with no corners or straight edges. Every point on the edge of a circle is the same distance from its center. Coins, wheels, and clocks are shaped like circles. A circle looks the same from every direction. A circle is not a square.

[user]what is a square?
[assistant]A square is a flat shape with four equal sides and four right-angle corners. All four sides of a square are the same length. Tiles, windows, and some boxes are shaped like squares. A square always has four equal sides and four equal corners. A square is not a circle.

[user]what is a triangle?
[assistant]A triangle is a flat shape with three sides and three corners. The three corners of a triangle are called angles. Triangles can be different sizes, but they always have exactly three sides. Rooftops and slices of pizza are often shaped like triangles. A triangle is not a square.

[user]what is a rectangle?
[assistant]A rectangle is a flat shape with four sides and four right-angle corners. The opposite sides of a rectangle are equal in length. Doors, books, and screens are often shaped like rectangles. A rectangle is like a square, but its sides are not all the same length. A rectangle is not a triangle.
